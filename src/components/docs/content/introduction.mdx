<h3 className="text-lg font-semibold mb-2.5 tracking-wide">Philosophy & Inspiration</h3>
<div className="mb-8 text-[0.9rem] mt-1">
    Sometimes the best way to learn a component is to build it properly, once. kiwi-ui grew out of a weekly practice of exploring micro-interactions, each component written with care for accessibility, composability, and the kind of subtle motion that makes an interface feel alive. The library is intentionally growing over time, with every addition held to the same standard as the last. <br/><br/>
    kiwi-ui is built on top of shadcn, inspired by libraries like shadcn itself and Spell UI, taking their foundation of clean, composable primitives and extending it with a layer of motion and personality that makes components feel crafted, not assembled.
</div>
<h3 className="text-lg font-semibold mb-2.5 tracking-wide">Why Kiwi?</h3>
<div className="mb-5 text-[0.9rem]">
Kiwi components don't always look like what you'd expect, and that's the point. Some components follow familiar patterns. Others push against them, exploring interactions and motion that feel fresh without feeling foreign. The throughline is always the same: simple where it counts, alive where it matters. <br/><br/>
Under the hood, kiwi-ui is built with React, Tailwind CSS, and Framer Motion — zero-config installation via the CLI, with components designed to be dropped into any codebase and scaled from day one.
</div>

<h3 className="text-lg font-semibold mb-3 tracking-wide">Who is it for?</h3>
<div className="mb-8 text-[0.9rem]">
kiwi-ui is for developers and designers who believe the details make the difference. If you want a library that ships predictable, accessible components but isn't afraid to do things a little differently, you'll feel right at home. 
It's not a replacement for shadcn or meant to rival it, it's what you reach for when you want to go further. Not to distract the user, but to make them feel like the interface was built specifically for them.
</div>
